Lawrence Edward Page (born March 26, 1973) is an American computer scientist and internet entrepreneur. He is best known as one of the co-founders of Google, along with Sergey Brin. Page was the chief executive officer (CEO) of Google from 1997 until August 2001, and again from April 2011 until July 2015, and subsequently the CEO of Alphabet Inc. until December 2019. Page is the co-creator and namesake of PageRank, a search ranking algorithm for Google, which he wrote with Brin. He received the Marconi Prize in 2004 with Brin for their work in transforming the accessibility of information. Page's architectural vision for PageRank and search engines laid the foundation for modern web indexing and search technologies.
