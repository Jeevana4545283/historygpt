Mark Elliot Zuckerberg (born May 14, 1984) is an American internet entrepreneur and philanthropist. He co-founded the social media website Facebook (now Meta Platforms) in 2004 with his Harvard roommates. Initially designed for college campuses, Facebook expanded rapidly to become the largest social network in the world. Zuckerberg took the company public in May 2012, and in 2021, rebranded the parent company to Meta Platforms to focus on the metaverse and AI technologies. Zuckerberg has pioneered social graph architectures, feed personalization algorithms, and large-scale web services, establishing new paradigms for global communication and network infrastructure.
